Kitfo: Ethiopians traditional food

What is Kitfo?

Kitfo is a traditional Ethiopian dish made from raw or lightly cooked minced beef, seasoned with spices. Itâ€™s typically mixed with mitmita (a blend of chili powder and spices) and niter kibbeh (a clarified butter infused with herbs like cardamom, garlic, and koseret, a type of Ethiopian herb).

Ingredients to make Kitfo:

  • Beef (usually lean cuts like sirloin or tenderloin) - 500 grams, finely minced or ground
  • Niter Kibbeh (spiced clarified butter) - 1/4 cup (or to taste
  • Mitmita
  • Salt
  • Injera or kocho

Steps to make Kitfo

  1. Prepare the Niter Kibbeh (if not already made):

    Melt unsalted butter over low heat and simmer with garlic, ginger, cardamom, koseret, and turmeric until fragrant.Strain and set aside.

  2. Prepare the Beef:

    Finely mince or grind the beef to the desired texture.

  3. Season the Kitfo: In a bowl, mix the beef with mitmita and salt. Add the warm niter kibbeh, combining thoroughly until all beef is coated and seasoned to your preference.

  4. Serve:

    Serve kitfo raw (tire), slightly warmed (leb leb), or fully cooked (betam leb leb) with ayib and injera or kocho on the side.
